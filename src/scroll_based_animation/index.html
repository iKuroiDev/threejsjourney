TORUS

A torus geometry is like a donut or a tire shape, with a curved surface that wraps around to form a loop. It looks like a round tube with a hole in the middle. The torus is interesting because it has continuous curvature and symmetry when you rotate it. It's used in math, physics, computer graphics, and design. People find it fascinating to study and play with because of its cool shape and properties.

CONE

A cone geometry is like an ice cream cone or a traffic cone shape. It has a pointed tip and a circular base, with a smooth curved surface that tapers from the base to the tip. Cones are used in architecture, engineering, and math. They're stable and efficient in distributing forces, making them versatile in design and fluid dynamics. Cones, whether as treats or geometric concepts, capture our attention with their simple yet impactful shape.

TORUS KNOT

A torus knot geometry is like a gnarly tangle of a rope or a funky piece of jewelry. It's formed by looping a rope around a torus shape and then tying it in intricate patterns. These knots have a mesmerizing appearance, with their loops and twists intertwining in a captivating manner. Torus knots come in different varieties, each defined by the number of times the rope wraps around the torus. They're not just cool to look at; they also have practical applications in fields like physics, chemistry, and even computer graphics.
